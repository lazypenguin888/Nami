Responsive Design

Nami Providence Restaurant Webpage

Original Webpage

Choice

The website I decided to choose was a restaurant webapage, link: Nami.
I chose this one to analyze and redesign as I felt that on a base level the webpage is very hard to digest, features of the webpage are unnoticeable, and some features are redudant or not working.

Problems
Significant Design Issues

  • Bad contrast: the text is dark grey while background is black, which makes the text really hard to read
  • Legibility: The stylish subtitles/title font text is hard to read, letters are too thick and hard to distinguish
  • Bad alignment from hometitle to content of page, it almost overlaps with very little space. It appears as if the logo is NamiHome instead of Nami
  • Grouping - closure: For the customer reviews, there is no form of closure for each review which makes it hard to see each review as its own
  • For header menu/buttons, it does not make a lot of sense to have buttons and then a dropdown menu for the remaining categories, especially when the displayed buttons are not the most important ("food/drink menu" would be more important than "buy gift cards").
  • Hierarchy: The major hierachy for text font sizes is 3 groups. It is confusing as the group 1 is the titles, then group 2 subtitles or comments/quotes (which is confusing), then group 3 of body text. Furthermore, the group 2 comments/quotes are usually placed after group 3 text which can be confusing.
  • Distinction: There isn't enough distinction between many different cateogries, its too monotone. For example the different titles/subtitles, different blocks of the page, and hyperlinks are almost completely blended into the text with no distinction.
  • Unresponsive page for different layouts: For mobile version or smaller windows the header buttons/menu overlaps other parts of the text

Usability

There are issues where some buttons do not work. For example the (hours & location) button does not direct to right place, it does nothing. Some features would be missed, for example its almost impossible to tell which text are hyperlinks due to no distinction.

Learnability

Learnability is a problem for the header buttons/menu. How they operate is confusing as there are redudant buttons which are the (about) and (hours & location) which are almost identical to the function of the (home) button, additionally they do not work which makes it even more confusing to understand.

Memorability

Memorability would be a significant issue with a major factor being that everything is too monotone. With no distinction its hard to piece out different parts of the webpage to remember. Also everything is really hard to read, so its hard for the user to take a snapshot of the webpage in their head. Additionally, inconjuction to learnability with some functions confusing to learn, it would be hard for the user to remember how to properly navigate through the functions immmediately upon next use.

Accessibility

Using the WebAIM WAVE to detect possible accessibility problems on the webpage, the issues are mainly contrast errors and alerts. I with both of them as contrast is definitely a major issues throughout the page, and for the alerts with skipped heading level, it is confusing as I mentioned earlier about Hierarchy in Significant Design Issues.
